about hyarajuku

HYARAJUKU is the stagename/business run by Mimi E., a queer artist born and raised in San Antonio, TX. After several years of trying to find an art medium they could really master, they discovered jewelry in late 2021. From there, they expanded their knowlege on color, proportion, design, and basic metalwork techniques while creating an array of emotionally-driven pieces.

The name came to be because of Mimi's lifelong admiration and appreciation for Harajuku subculture (a neighborhood in Japan, known for its eccentric fashion and acceptance of expression through it). HYARAJUKU takes heavy inspiration from the styles of the neighborhood, includiing infamous gothic lolita, a fashion subculture with influence from victorian-era gothic fashion.
A few other favorite sources of inspiration include: FRUiTS magazine, bunnies, Kuromi (Sanrio), the works of Ai Yazawa & Cocteau Twins, crosses, lace, and v-kei.
